# muzamilkhanrealtor.com — Redesign Plan (June 2026 audit)

## Verdict: refresh, don't rebuild

The current design is in good shape. Strong differentiated positioning (15 years in
construction, "I actually live here"), clean dark hero, clear Buy/Sell paths, Zillow
reviews with attribution, Learn hubs for buyers and sellers, contact form plus booking
links, newsletter capture. A full visual rebuild would burn weeks for marginal gain.

What's actually holding the site back is invisible: technical SEO, performance, and
stock imagery that undercuts the "local expert" brand. Fix those and the existing
design converts and ranks.

## What's working (keep)

- Positioning and copy voice. "Most agents can't do that math. I can." is the best
  line on the site. Don't let a redesign sand this off.
- Information architecture: Buy / Sell / Learn / Neighborhoods / Blog / About / Contact.
- Schema.org RealEstateAgent JSON-LD with DRE#, areaServed, credentials. Most agent
  sites don't have this.
- Real sitemap.xml, Google site verification (Search Console likely connected).
- Zillow reviews with names, locations, dates.

## Issues, in priority order

### P0 — SEO-critical (these gate every traffic effort)

1. **Client-side rendered SPA.** The site is a Vite/React app serving an empty
   `<div id="root">`. Every page returns the *same* homepage title and meta
   description. Blog posts, neighborhood pages, Buy/Sell pages have no per-page
   titles, descriptions, OG tags, or article schema in the HTML. Google renders JS
   eventually but ranks per-page metadata; Bing is weaker at it; and AI assistants
   (ChatGPT, Perplexity — a growing source of "find me a realtor in El Cerrito"
   answers) mostly read raw HTML and currently see an empty page.
   **Fix: prerender to static HTML at build time** (vite-plugin-ssr / vite prerender
   plugin, or react-helmet + a prerender step) with unique title/meta/OG/JSON-LD per
   route. This is the single highest-leverage change on the entire list.
2. **robots.txt missing** — the SPA catch-all returns the homepage HTML for
   /robots.txt. Serve a real one pointing at the sitemap.
3. **No analytics.** No GA4/gtag in the bundle. You cannot improve what you can't
   measure. Add GA4 + events on the CTAs (call, email, book consult, form submit,
   guide download) and confirm Search Console is connected.

### P1 — Performance

4. **~25 Google Font families load on every page** (Architects Daughter, Fira Code,
   JetBrains Mono, Oxanium... a Replit Agent artifact). The site uses maybe two.
   Cut the fonts link to just the families actually used, `display=swap`. This is
   hurting LCP/Core Web Vitals, which feeds rankings.
5. Hero images should be optimized (WebP/AVIF, properly sized, `loading="eager"`
   only for the hero, lazy elsewhere).

### P2 — Brand/imagery (the visible "redesign")

6. **Stock photos contradict the brand.** The whole pitch is hyper-local
   authenticity, then: Buy page hero is a generic stucco suburban tract home that
   reads Sacramento/Texas, not East Bay; Blog hero is a marina; Neighborhoods hero is
   a generic skyline; Sell hero is a gray gradient. Replace with real, recognizable
   East Bay photography: El Cerrito hills streets, craftsman and midcentury homes,
   BART station, the bay view from Kensington, Solano Ave. Shoot these on a phone in
   one golden-hour afternoon — imperfect-but-real beats stock here, and the photos
   double as Instagram/GBP content.
7. **Headshot integration.** The hero photo is good; add a smaller version near the
   contact CTAs ("talk to a person" effect).

### P3 — Conversion upgrades

8. **Home valuation lead magnet.** "Get a Free Valuation" should land on a dedicated
   2-step form (address → contact info) framed as "a real number from someone who
   knows what repairs cost, not a Zestimate." Highest-intent seller capture there is.
9. **Gate the First-Time Buyer Guide** as a PDF download for an email address —
   feeds the newsletter and FUB nurture.
10. **Sticky mobile CTA bar** (Call / Text / Book) — most visitors will be on phones.
11. **More social proof.** Only 2 reviews shown; pull 4-6 from Zillow, and once
    Google reviews exist, mix those in. Add transaction count if favorable.
12. **Blog dates create a staleness signal** — the Jan 2025 → Apr 2026 gap is
    visible. Either backfill 2025 with 2-3 evergreen posts or de-emphasize dates on
    listing cards (keep them on posts).
